<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Reroll System & Core Mechanics">

Understanding the trait system is essential for progressing through high-end challenges, boss fights, and endless trials. In the game, every summoned unit receives a random trait that alters its base statistics, such as range, damage output, attack speed, or placement cost. Players use Reroll Resources (RR) to swap these traits, hoping to land powerful multipliers that elevate a standard unit into a meta carry.

While Gems are used to summon new characters, RR is the designated currency for modifying existing characters. Managing this economy early dictates how quickly you can transition from clearing basic story stages to dominating raid challenges.

| Resource | Type | Main Source | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| RR | Reroll Resource | Codes, challenges, milestones | Critical |
| Gems | Summon Currency | Story clearing, daily quests | High |
| Cubes | Upgrade Material | planet Namak, boss challenges | Medium |
| Malice | Upgrade Material | codes, event milestones | Medium |

<Callout type="success" title="Mechanic Tip">
Traits apply immediately upon rolling and scale as your unit levels up and undergoes evolution. Rolling a high-tier trait on a base unit carries over when you evolve them, making early investments in top-tier units highly efficient.
</Callout>

To optimize your team's utility, focus your rerolls on characters that fit your squad's primary damage-dealing roles. Support and farm units require different setups, meaning you must identify the unit's role before spending valuable RR.

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Reroll Workflow">

Executing a proper rerolling strategy prevents resource depletion. Beginners often make the mistake of using RR on temporary Rare or Epic units. Follow this structured progression path to maximize the value of your reroll resources.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Claim active promo codes">
    Before interacting with the summon or reroll systems, open the codes panel on the right side of the screen. Redeem the codes `Release!` and `SorryForDelay` to claim starter Gems, RR, Cubes, and Malice.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assemble a foundation squad">
    Spend your initial Gems on the active banners to secure a working team. Do not worry about traits yet. Your immediate goal is to acquire at least one reliable DPS carry, preferably a Mythic or Legendary unit.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Locate the trait reroll area">
    Navigate to the reroll station in the main lobby. Interact with the interface and place your chosen unit into the slot. This screen displays your current RR balance and the unit's active trait.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Evaluate unit viability">
    Only spend RR if the unit is a long-term investment, such as Kizuki or Queen. If you are using temporary filler units, accept whatever default trait they have to save your RR for later.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Perform targeted rerolls">
    Roll traits individually. Keep an eye out for top-tier combat traits like Divine or Stellar. Stop rolling as soon as you hit a trait that matches the unit's utility.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Warning">
Farming RR takes time. Do not chase perfect rolls on Legendary units when you plan to replace them with Mythic or Secret units. Settle for average traits like Jack of All during the early game.
</Callout>

Once your core units possess competent traits, you can confidently push through harder story stages and unlock advanced challenges, which in turn yield more upgrade materials.

</Section>

Use the following reference tables to determine when to keep a trait and which trait works best for the game's top-performing characters.

| Trait | Tier | Key Benefits | Target Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Divine | S-Tier | High-end damage boost, overall stat amplification | Secret and Mythic DPS carries |
| Stellar | S-Tier | Damage-over-time amplification, shield pressure | DoT and shield breakers |
| Sniper | A-Tier | Crit chance scaling, single-target damage increase | Crit carries, boss killers |
| Tycoon | Utility | Income generation, placement cost efficiency | Support and farm units |
| Jack of All | B-Tier | Balanced stats, minor all-around improvement | Early Legendary units |

| Unit | Rarity | Role | Ideal Trait | Alternative |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Kizuki | Secret | DPS, freeze utility, shield breaker | Divine | Stellar |

| Blood | Mythic | Boss killer, crit scaling | Sniper | Divine |

<Callout type="tip" title="Optimization Tip">
Units with damage-over-time (DoT) mechanics, such as Ashura Swordsman (3SS) or Insect Slayer (Poison), benefit significantly from Stellar. This trait enhances their damage ticks, making it competitive with Divine for specific modes.
</Callout>

If you roll Tycoon on a damage carry, continue rerolling. Similarly, if you roll Divine on an economy unit, it is usually best to reroll for Tycoon to maximize your money generation phase during complex maps.
