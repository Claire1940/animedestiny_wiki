<Section icon={Shield} title="Trait Optimization and Reroll Guide">

Traits act as passive modifiers that can drastically alter a unit's performance. Rerolling traits requires Reroll Shards (RR), which are scarce resources. Therefore, you should avoid spending RR on temporary units and save them for your long-term S-tier carries.

Matching the correct trait to a unit's role is crucial. For instance, putting a support trait like Tycoon on a primary DPS unit like Kizuki will severely limit your squad's damage potential.

| Trait / Resource | Type | Best For | Action Advice |
|------------------|------|----------|---------------|
| **RR (Reroll Shard)** | Resource | Trait Rerolls | Save for Mythic and Secret units. Avoid wasting on Rares. |
| **Divine** | Trait | High-End DPS Carries | Keep immediately. Provides the highest overall damage multiplier. |
| **Stellar** | Trait | DoT and Shield Breakers | Great on Ashura Swordsman or Insect Slayer. |
| **Sniper** | Trait | Crit-Focused DPS | Best on Eternal Night and Radiant Hunter for single-target boss damage. |
| **Tycoon** | Trait | Economy / Support Units | Keep on units like Bulm or Cyan Idol to boost money generation. |
| **Jack of All** | Trait | Beginner Legendaries | Acceptable early roll, but do not spend heavy RR chasing it. |
| **Cubes** | Resource | Progression | Collect from codes and use to upgrade unit stats in the lobby. |
| **Malice** | Resource | Progression | Claim from code bundles to unlock specialized unit upgrades. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Warning">
Do not use your Reroll Shards on Legendary or Epic units. The stat differences between these tiers and Mythic/Secret tiers mean your RR is always better spent on higher-rarity characters.
</Callout>

</Section>
