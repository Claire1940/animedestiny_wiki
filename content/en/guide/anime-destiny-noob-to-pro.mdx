<Section icon={Shield} title="Trait Optimization: Reroll Strategy Guide">

Traits act as passive modifiers that can completely change a unit's effectiveness. When you roll a unit, it comes with a random trait. By using Reroll Shards (RR), you can change this trait to better suit the unit's role in your squad. 

Understanding when to keep a trait and when to reroll is essential for maintaining a healthy resource economy. Chasing a specific legendary trait on a temporary unit is a common pitfall that can leave you resource-starved when you finally pull a Secret unit.

| Trait / Resource | Type | Ideal Match | Reroll Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Divine | Trait | High-end DPS & Secret carries | Keep immediately; do not reroll under any circumstances |
| Stellar | Trait | DoT & Shield-breaking Mythics | Excellent for Ashura Swordsman or Insect Slayer; keep |
| Sniper | Trait | Crit-focused single-target units | Keep on boss killers like Eternal Night |
| Tycoon | Trait | Economy & Support units | Keep on farm units like Bulm or Cyan Idol |
| Jack of All | Trait | Starter Legendary units | Accept on temporary units; do not waste RR optimizing them |
| RR (Reroll Shards) | Resource | Mythic & Secret units | Save the bulk of this resource for end-game carries |

<Callout type="tip" title="Reroll Efficiency Tip">
Only use your RR on units that you plan to keep in your main squad for a long time. A Mythic unit with a mediocre trait is still generally stronger than a Legendary unit with a perfect trait.
</Callout>

</Section>
