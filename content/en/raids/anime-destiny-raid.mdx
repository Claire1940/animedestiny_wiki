<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Raid Execution Strategy">

Successfully navigating an **anime destiny raid** requires precise placement timing and economic management. Players must focus on generating income during the initial waves, transition to defensive setups mid-game, and concentrate raw DPS on the boss during the final wave.

Follow these steps to structure your gameplay during a raid match:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish Early Economy">
    Place your economy units, such as Bulm, near the back of the map where they are safe from early leaks. Upgrade them immediately during the first three waves to maximize your cash generation for the rest of the round.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Deploy Chokepoint Defense">
    Position your primary DPS units at key corners or chokepoints where their attack range covers multiple paths. Place a cheap shield breaker near the entrance to strip enemy defenses before they reach your main damage zone.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Coordinate Upgrades">
    Prioritize upgrading your main carry unit (e.g., Frozen Moon or Founding Titan) to unlock their area-of-effect attacks. Avoid spreading your money evenly across multiple low-tier units; one max-level carry is more effective than five low-level units.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Focus the Boss Shield">
    When the raid boss spawns, shift your focus to upgrading your shield breakers. Once the boss shield is depleted, activate any unit-specific active abilities to burst down the boss's remaining health pool before it reaches the exit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Execution Tip">
Use the free Auto Roll feature in the lobby to secure duplicates of your core units. Having duplicates allows you to upgrade unit stats in the preparation menu before entering the raid lobby.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Trait Rerolling & Optimization">

Traits significantly alter a unit's performance in the **anime destiny raid** mode. Using Reroll Shards (RR) allows you to replace weak traits with high-tier modifiers that scale your unit's damage, range, or utility. Players should avoid wasting RR on temporary Legendary or Rare units, saving these valuable resources for Secret and Mythic carries.

The table below lists the primary traits available in the game and how they perform in raid scenarios.

| Trait Name | Type | Best Fit | Raid Utility | Reroll Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Divine | Attack / Speed | S-Tier Carries | Increases overall DPS by a massive margin | High |
| Stellar | DoT / Range | DoT Carries | Enhances damage-over-time and shield pressure | High |
| Sniper | Crit / Range | Single-Target | Ideal for boss-focused damage dealers | Medium |
| Tycoon | Economy | Support Units | Increases wave income for faster upgrades | Medium |
| Jack of All | Balanced | Starter Units | Decent stat boost for early progression | Low |

<Callout type="warning" title="Reroll Trap">
Do not chase a perfect Divine trait on units you plan to replace. Accept utility traits like Sniper or Stellar on mid-tier Mythics, and save your bulk RR reserves for Secret units.
</Callout>

</Section>
