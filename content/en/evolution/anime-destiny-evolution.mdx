<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Anime Destiny Evolution Mechanics Explained">

Evolving units is the single most important power spike in the game. Using the **anime destiny evolution** system allows you to take base Mythic and Secret units and transform them into their awakened forms, unlocking new skill kits, scaling their damage, and improving utility. In this tower defense experience, base units often struggle against shield mechanics and late-game boss stages. Evolved forms remedy this by dramatically shifting their base multipliers and introducing screen-clearing Area of Effect attacks.

To initiate an evolution, you must visit the designated evolution area in the main lobby. The system requires a combination of the base character at any level, a set amount of Gold, and specific materials gathered from challenges and boss trials. Unlike some tower defense games where level progress resets upon transformation, your character level remains intact, carrying over all accumulated experience to their newly unlocked form.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Anime Destiny Evolution Guide">

Evolving a unit is a permanent process that consumes your hard-earned materials. Following a precise preparation routine will prevent you from wasting resources on the wrong units or executing an upgrade before your squad is ready to support it.

Use the steps below to successfully execute your first evolution.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Check Unit Compatibility">
    Confirm that the unit in your inventory is eligible for evolution. Currently, only select Mythic and Secret characters possess evolved states. Check the evolution pedestal to view the exact requirements.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Gather Base Materials">
    Farm the required amount of Stat Cubes, Malice, and character-specific shards. Do not attempt an evolution until you have the exact amounts, as partial submissions are not supported.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Prepare Your Team Economy">
    Ensure you have support units like Bulm or Cyan Idol leveled up. Evolved carries cost more to place and upgrade during active battles, requiring a stronger early-wave economy to deploy.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute at the Pedestal">
    Navigate to the lobby pedestal, select your target unit, confirm the material consumption, and lock in the evolved form.
  </Step>
</Steps>

After completing the evolution, your unit's compatibility with high-tier traits changes. Evolved units scale dramatically with percentage-based stat modifiers, making it the perfect time to spend your saved Trait Rerolls.

| Trait Name | Base Effect | Evolved Synergy | Best Target Units |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Divine | +Damage & Range | Scales summon/AOE damage | Kizuki, Queen, Shadow Monarch |
| Stellar | DoT Tick rate boost | Accelerates bleed and burn | Ashura Swordsman, Insect Slayer |
| Sniper | +Crit Rate / Range | Amplifies boss single-target DPS | Eternal Night, Radiant Hunter |
| Tycoon | +Money Generation | Increases farm unit yields | Bulm, Cyan Idol |

Rerolling traits on base units is generally inefficient. Save your Trait Rerolls (RR) for post-evolution characters to maximize the value of top-tier rolls like Divine or Stellar.

<Callout type="info" title="Trait Management Advice">
If your unit rolls a decent utility trait like Sniper early on, keep it. Do not chase a perfect Divine roll until you have successfully evolved your core damage dealers and secured a stable placement team.
</Callout>
