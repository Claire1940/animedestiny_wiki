<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Traits & Reroll Strategy">

Traits are passive multipliers applied to units when they are summoned. You can alter a unit's trait using Reroll Shards (RR) in the game lobby. Matching the correct trait to a character's role is crucial for maximizing performance in high-level trials.

| Trait or Resource | Type | Optimal Application | Strategic Advice |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Reroll Shards (RR) | Currency | Modifying unit traits | Save for Secret and Mythic carries |
| Divine | Trait | High-end DPS & main carries | Keep immediately; provides top stat buffs |
| Stellar | Trait | DoT & shield-breaking units | Excellent for units like Ashura Swordsman |
| Sniper | Trait | Crit-scaling boss killers | Best on single-target units like Eternal Night |
| Tycoon | Trait | Economy & support characters | Keep on farm units like Bulm or Cyan Idol |
| Jack of All | Trait | Temporary starter units | Acceptable on early Legendaries; do not reroll |

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Warning">
Reroll resources are scarce. Spending RR on Rare, Epic, or standard Legendary units is generally inefficient since these units are replaced during mid-game progression.
</Callout>

### Trait Optimization Guidelines
When deciding whether to reroll a trait, evaluate the unit's primary function in your squad. For example, damage dealers require offensive traits like **Divine** or **Stellar** to increase their multiplier output. Support units, especially those that generate money or buff others, benefit far more from utility traits like **Tycoon**, which amplifies resource generation rates.
